After.js

After.js comes from seasoned React developer Jared Palmer, also the brains behind Razzle, who was impressed by Next.js overall, but frustrated by some of it's architectural choices. His main gripe was with it's homegrown routing system, which is not the de-facto standard React Router 4.In addition to giving credit to the brilliance of the simplicity of the concepts behind Next.js, such as the getInitialProps method. But the After.js dismisses the idea of relying on the file system as an API, which is one of the core concepts of Next borrowed from PHP development. In addition to taking ideas and the routing component off-the-shelf, After.js also is using a fork of Razzle to provide it's universal capabilities. Razzle is a decoupled SSR toolkit that works across frameworks
